Mission and Vision

  • Mission

    To rescue, rehabilitate, reintegrate and re-socialize street boys.

  • Vision

    To ensure that every street boy is rescued, rehabilitated and reintegrated into the community and/or family sustainably.

Make an Impact Now!

Objective - Prevention and Economic Empowerment

  • Facilitation of Prevention programs within communities, while rescues are conducted directly from the streets there is need to have gate keeping structures that will intervene in the event a child is at risk of ending up in the streets. This will ensure children are shielded from having to experience the unforgiving street life and minimize the number of children opting to go to the streets.

  • Where appropriately possible, facilitation towards economic independence of families: This will enable families to holistically and equally provide for all the children in a given household.

  • Targets for empowerment will mostly include families of children that have been reintegrated and those under our prevention program. Home assessments to establish eligibility shall be conducted

Objective - Re-integrate

  • Facilitation of reintegration for the rehabilitated children with their families after they are located by the Nyumba Ya Tumaini staff through the Home Tracing program.
  • Facilitation of access to education and vocational training for children reintegrated with their families and those under NYT long term programs
  • Creation of Networks for collaboration and referral opportunities in relation to medical services for children who require long term care as well as offering long term institutional care.

Objective - Re-Socialize

  • Empowering the family to meet the needs of all the children in the nuclear family, keep them all together and minimize separation of siblings.
  • Connecting the child to community social programs such as schools, churches, recreational facilities, health centers
  • Engaging and involving the community throughout reintegration of the child so they are central to the process of smooth transition of the child within the community.
